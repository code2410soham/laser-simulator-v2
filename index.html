π DashBoard Mathematical Formulas

How we obtain real-time metrics in this physical simulation.

1. Intensity Calculation

I = (Np / Nmax) × 100

Where Np is the current photon count and Nmax is the saturation limit (500 photons).

2. Coherence Factor

C = α × (L / D)

Alignment factor (α) proportional to mirror stability and cavity length (L) vs divergence (D).

3. Beam Directionality

α > 95% Collimated
α > 70% Divergent
α < 70% Scattered

Calculated based on the angular spread relative to the optical axis.

4. Photon Density

ρ = Np / (W × H)

The number of active photons per unit area of the laser tube.

Core Principles

Bridging the gap between theory and virtual experimentation.

1. Stimulated Emission

The cornerstone of laser operation. An incident photon of specific energy interacts with an excited atom, prompting it to release an identical second photon. This produces a coherent stream of light where all photons share the same phase and direction.

Coherence Amplification
✨

2. Population Inversion

Achieving a state where more atoms occupy the excited state than the ground state. This is essential because, under normal conditions, absorption would outweigh emission. We use external energy (pumping) to tip the scales.

Energy State Meta-stable

He-Ne Laser System

The Helium-Neon laser uses a gas mixture where Helium acts as the excitation carrier. Through inter-atomic collisions, energy is transferred to Neon atoms, causing them to lase at a stable 632.8 nm red beam.

Gas Mixture: 10:1 (He:Ne)
