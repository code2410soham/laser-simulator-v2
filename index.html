Core Principles & Theory

Understanding the fundamental physics and engineering of Laser systems.

1. Basic Principles of Laser

Spontaneous Emission: An atom in an excited state returns to a lower state naturally, emitting a photon in a random direction and phase.

Stimulated Emission: The key to laser action. An external photon triggers an excited atom to emit a second photon that is identical in phase, frequency, and direction.

Population Inversion: Achieving a state where the number of atoms in a higher energy level exceeds those in the lower level. This ensures that stimulation dominates over absorption.

2. Elements of a Laser System

Active Medium

The substance (gas, liquid, solid) where population inversion takes place. E.g., Ruby crystal, He-Ne gas mixture.

Pumping Source

The energy supplier that excites the atoms to higher energy levels. Methods include optical, electrical, or chemical pumping.

Optical Resonator

A pair of mirrors (one fully reflecting, one partially) that bounce photons back and forth to sustain amplification.

4. He-Ne Laser (Gas Laser)

The Helium-Neon (He-Ne) laser is a popular continuous-wave gas laser. It typically emits red light at 632.8 nm. The gas mixture consists of about 10 parts Helium and 1 part Neon at low pressure.

The Process: Electrical discharge excites Helium atoms to metastable states. These Helium atoms then collide with Neon atoms, transferring energy and creating a population inversion in Neon, which leads to laser emission.

He-Ne is highly stable and used extensively in alignment, holography, and barcode scanning.

Real-World Applications

How Laser technology transforms modern industries and life.

🏥

Medical Applications

  • • Surgery: Bloodless scalpel for precise incisions.
  • • Ophthalmology: Correcting vision (LASIK) and retinal repair.
  • • Dermatology: Tattoo removal and skin resurfacing.
🏭

Industrial Applications

  • • Precision Cutting: High-speed cutting of metals and fabrics.
  • • Laser Welding: Strong, localized bonding for automotive/aerospace.
  • • 3D Printing: SLS (Selective Laser Sintering) for complex parts.
🌌

Holography-Recording

  • • Principle: Recording the interference pattern of laser light.
  • • Usage: Securing ID cards and currency.
  • • Future: Truly 3D cinematic and educational displays.
